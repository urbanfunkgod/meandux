Electrified Trucking Journey

Welcome to my journey with Hyliion, where innovation meets practicality in commercial trucking. Over two exciting years, I played a pivotal role as the lead engineer in software design, focusing on creating the Hyliion ERX's Copilot software. This cutting-edge Human-Machine Interface (HMI) empowers vehicle operators to seamlessly monitor and control the electric aspects of the ERX system, marking a significant leap in trucking technology.

Simultaneously, I architected and designed Hyliion's comprehensive fleet management system, HUB. This system, which I meticulously defined and built, serves dual purposes: catering to various internal engineering requirements and providing an external, customer-facing platform. Through this, I have helped bridge the gap between intricate engineering and user-centric design, ensuring that internal teams and external users benefit from a coherent, efficient, and intuitive experience.

Role

A multifaceted and deeply integrated within the High-Level Software (HLS) engineering team. I supported two key delivery teams. One focused on the vehicle HMI, and the other on the fleet management platform.

Problem

The Hyliion project addresses critical challenges in the trucking industry, focusing on improving fuel efficiency, reducing emissions, and enhancing the overall driver experience.

Solution

Our solution integrates modular UX design, data visualization, and analysis of our exceptional cloud data from our innovative electric powertrain technology, providing truck drivers and fleet operators a seamless and eco-friendly product experience.

Establishing Foundations for Harmonious Digital Experiences

At Hyliion, I focused on three key goals: redefining user experience, establishing robust UX practices, and shifting from a technology-centric to a user-centric design philosophy. This approach led to the creation of intuitive, accessible, and advanced digital products, significantly enhancing user experiences and advancing our software design approach in the commercial trucking industry.

Defining and Advocating User-Centered Design Recognizing the need for a shift to User-Centered Design at Hyliion, I championed the UCD approach in software development, clarifying the essence of UX and advocating for designs driven by user needs and behaviors.

Establishing a Scalable Design Framework I established a scalable design language and system at Hyliion, setting the foundation for a unified and adaptable user experience across all digital interfaces. This strategy was crucial for the consistent and flexible evolution of our future digital products.

Implementing a Comprehensive User Research Program I initiated a comprehensive user research program at Hyliion to validate design assumptions, starting with internal test drivers and expanding to include customer feedback during fleet trials. Employing methodologies like contextual inquiries, usability studies, and interviews, this program provided essential insights for product development and emphasized the importance of regular, early engagement with end-users for direct feedback.

My work in user-centered design and research at Hyliion laid the groundwork for key product developments, such as the ERX's Copilot software, which facilitated drivers' transition from ICEs to electric power, and the HUB fleet management system, enhancing vehicle tracking and maintenance. These innovations have positioned Hyliion as a leader in sustainable trucking, simplifying complex technologies into intuitive interfaces that improve daily operations and support environmental conservation.

Project FAQs

  • The Copilot software significantly enhances the trucking experience by providing an intuitive interface for vehicle operators to monitor and control the electric aspects of the ERX system. Its design focuses on ease of use, ensuring that all vital information and controls are easily accessible, improving operational efficiency. The software also includes features for optimizing energy consumption, contributing to reduced emissions and increased overall fleet performance.

  • UX is critical in promoting environmental sustainability in the trucking industry. Our design has made it easier for drivers and fleet managers to understand and manage their carbon footprint. Our software provides actionable insights and suggestions, like optimal drive modes and speed adjustments, directly contributing to reducing emissions. Additionally, by clearly communicating these environmental efforts to customers, we help foster a culture of sustainability within the industry.

  • Balancing technical requirements with user-friendly design involves a deep understanding of both the technological aspects and the end-user needs. We achieved this balance by engaging in extensive user research to understand what truck drivers and fleet managers genuinely need. This led to the development of interfaces that were feature-rich and intuitive. Regular feedback loops and prototype testing were vital in refining the UX to ensure it met technical standards while remaining accessible and easy to use.
